Pho Tai

Description

Pho is a traditional vietnamese soup and noodle dish that is also enjoyed in other south east asian countries. It is comprised of a broth, rice noodles, herbs/vegetable, various types of meat, and condiments such as sriracha and houisin sauce. Traditionally, the broth is made by boiling and simmering beef bones and oxtail over a long time to make a bone broth. The hot broth is then poured over rice noodles and raw, thinly sliced pieces of beef. The heat of the broth cooks the beef and loosens the noodles to eating texture. The dish is then topped with the other aforementioned toppings.

There are various types of pho which are usually characterized by the type of broth as well as the toppings included in the dish. For example, pho dac biet or "combination pho" is simply pho with every possible topping available including things like tripe, meatballs, brisket, thin beef, etc. Pho ga or chicken Pho is pho whose broth and meat toppings are made with chicken instead of beef. The following recipe makes pho tai or pho with only thin beef. However, feel free to add whatever toppings or condiments you want to the recipe since most of those additions are added at plating time and is key reason why pho is so modular and customizeable.

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds beef bones
  • 1 onion, peeled and cut in half
  • 5 slices of fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 2 pods of star anise
  • 2 sticks of cinamon
  • 2 and half tablespoons fish sauce
  • 4 qts water
  • 8 ounces of dried rice noodles
  • 1 and half pounds beef top sirloin, thinly sliced
  • half cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon chopped green onion
  • 1 and half cups bean sprouts
  • 1 bunch Thai basil
  • 1 lime
  • housin sauce
  • sriracha/any garilic chili sauce

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C)
  2. Roast beef bones on baking sheet until bones are browned. (Around an hour)
  3. Roast onion in baking sheet until soft and blackened. (Around 45 minutes)
  4. Place bones, onion, ginger, salt, star anise, cinamon, and fish sauce in a large stockpot and cover with 4 qts of water. Bring to boil and reduce heat to low until you reach a simmer. Simmer for 6 to 10 hours, removing any scum that rises in the mean time. When ready, strain a serving of soup into sauce pan and set aside.
  5. Place rice noodles in large bowl filled with room temperature water and allow to soak for 1 hour. Bring some water to a boil and place soaked noodles into boiling water for a minute. Remove and shock with ice.
  6. Bring broth to a rolling boil and divide noodles into bowls with desired serving size. Top bowls with beef slices, cilantro, and green onions. Pour hot broth into each bowl. Stir and let sit until the beef is partially cooked and no longer pink, 1 to 2 minutes. Add toppings such as bean sprouts, thai basil, lime wedges, and condiments on the side.
